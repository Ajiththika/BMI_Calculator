About BMI & Health

Understanding the Body Mass Index and why it matters.

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple screening tool widely used to identify potential weight problems in adults. It is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters (kg/m²).

While BMI does not measure body fat directly, it is strongly correlated with various metabolic and disease outcomes.

BMI Categories

Standard weight status categories associated with BMI ranges for adults:

BMI Range Status
Below 18.5 Underweight
18.5 – 24.9 Normal Weight
25.0 – 29.9 Overweight
30.0 and Above Obesity

Why it matters

Maintaining a healthy weight is important for overall health. Being overweight or obese increases the risk of health conditions such as hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and cardiovascular disease.

Conversely, being underweight can be associated with malnutrition, osteoporosis, and anemia.
